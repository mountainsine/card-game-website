MAGIC THE GATHERING - TYPE-4

TYPE-4 or the Fourth Type. This a fan-made format (similar to commander, noble, etc.) and unique. The name type-4 is a reference to old boomer Magic the Gathering when the official formats were Type 1 (all cards) and Type 2 (some cards).

HOW TO PLAY

YOU WILL NEED

  • Type-4 stack (Only 1 player needs this. See Below).
  • Minimum 2 players.
  • 5-35 Minutes per player.
  • Basic Understanding of the rules of Magic the Gathering.
  • Spin down dice.

RULES

  • Normal Magic the gathering rules e.g. Turns, Priority, Draw sequencing.
  • Singleton Format (only 1 of each card allowed).
  • Each player starts game with 30 life.
  • Each player shares the same Library.
  • Each player shares the same Graveyard.
  • Each player can ONLY play 1 spell per turn (inluding other players' turns).
  • Each player has unlimited mana of every color at all times.
  • Land cards are allowed in a type-4 stack to use for their abilities instead of mana generation.
  • All cards legal (see deck building rules below though!).
  • When the Library empties it flips.
  • Each player can win the game by bringing each other player's life total to 0.
  • Scales Up to 2-8 people.

DECK BUILDING

Type-4 is a neat game idea for going through bulk and finding a lot of fun low cost but high mana value cards that would not neccesarily be played otherwise. Stuff Too expensive (mana value in-game) for Commander and worthless in IRL money.

There are deckbuilding constraints in the format to prevent the game from going sideways. A more obvious example of this is the classic mtg card Fireball which would be an OTK in type-4 and is avoided (few want to win or lose that way).

AVOID

  • ❌ Spells that cost X or could go infinite.
  • ❌ Tutors.
  • ❌ Sorcery Spells.
  • ❌ Flashback.
  • ❌ Token generation.

LOOK FOR

  • ✅ High-cost instants
  • ✅ Ways to make the game more fun multiplayer!
  • ✅ High Cost draw spells.
  • ✅ Removal that tucks or put Permanents on top of the library (lol).
  • ✅ Cards that abuse converted mean cost down-sides.
  • ✅ Modal or utility instants.
  • ✅ A good mix of Removal and Counters versus Creatures and permanent spells.

Good Mechanics

  • ✅ Flash.
  • ✅ Suspend.
  • ✅ Bloodrush.
  • ✅ Cycling (does not count as a spell!).
  • ✅ Monstrosity (MONSTERS hell yes!).
  • ✅ Islandwalk (lol).
  • ✅ Entwine.
  • ✅ Splice onto Arcane.
  • ✅ Upkeep downside.

OPTIONAL CONSTRAINTS

  • No Cards over $1 Dollar.
  • Card picks to hit the player in the nostalgia e.g. rats, lightning bolt, etc.
  • Unglued cards. Avoid Infinite combos though!
  • Wheel Effects.
  • Old Card Art.
  • Bad cards or cards with missing upside (see the unicorn in my stack).
  • Vanilla creatures with cool card art.
  • No spells less than 6 mana.

So yeah, leave the good stuff for Modern, Historic, and EDH and bring your JANK to type-4. Enjoy!

UPGRADES

I'm trying to keep my Type-4 stack as economical as possible.

  • Keep it casual.
  • Anticipate spilled beer on the cards.
  • As soon as a card becomes over $1-5 in the stack it is sold. Some exceptions.
  • Ideally I'd prefer each card to be under $1
  • I lose stuff and want to feel comfortable throwing this in a backpack on the way to the LGS.
  • It's a jank format that can benefit from cuts more than others.
