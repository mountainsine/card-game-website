THE ECOSYSTEM OF CARD GAMES

Keyforge was and is methodone for former Magic the Gathering players. A game by Richard Garfield with a fresh take on the card game experience.

Less Deck Building, More Gaming.

Because of this it is impossible to talk about KEYFORGE without talking about games that exist(ed) before and after it.

The way Keyforge set out to fix the TCG experience was bold.

Problems:

❌ Frequent Erattas / Card Bannings.
❌ Pay-to-Win Incentives. *Cough* MTG *Cough*
❌ Land Flooding.
❌ Net Decking (Creating a stale meta).
❌ Rotation.
❌ Land..in general.

Strong selling points included:

✅ No Resource Management!
✅ No Counter Spells or Out-Of-Turn play!
✅ No Milling to death!
✅ Every Deck Uniquely Generated.

FORMATS

🔥 ARCHON

There is no constructed in keyforge but this is the closest thing. Think friday night magic "standard" in Magic the Gathering. In general "spike" or competitive players are drawn to this format.

  • Official Format
  • Each player brings a deck to use at the event
  • You get to see your opponents deck list pre-game
  • Best-Of-One (generally)
  • 35 min rounds
  • Chainbound (generally)

Keyforge decks can be really challenging to pilot so this has been the most enjoyable format for me because it allows the most practice ahead of tournament.


Because of the lack of deck-building and the scarcity/opinionated-ness around what makes a strong deck or player...some players may actually gravitate towards other formats like reversal or adaptive or even sealed (in general) playing to the game's spirit of working with whatever tools are in front of you.

🧠 ADAPTIVE

Best of 3 format iteration with chain bidding based on outcomes.

Each player brings an archon or sealed deck and plays normally in a best of 3.

For the second game, each player swaps decks with his or her opponent and plays using his or her opponent’s deck.

If the third game occurs, players bid chains to use the deck which won both of the previous games.

TRIAD

Pick 3 decks to use throughout the tournament.

Each round, each player chooses one of their opponent’s decks to “bench,” making that deck unusable for that match. Each round consists of a normal best-of-three match.

When a player wins a game, they must switch to their other deck for the rest of the match.

ADAPTIVE-SHORT

The exact same concept as ADAPTIVE but compressed to 1 game for time and competition. A Fun exercise in both deck-list evaluation and chain bidding.

RUSH

Sonic the hedgehog. Superman. A Speeding Bullet. Ferrari. These decks floor it and don't look back. This an interesting strategy for a number of reasons:

  • Make all other strategies irrelevant by simply being faster.
  • Requires Less Cognitive lift at a tournament (compared to a more board or trick heavy deck.
  • Wins Faster. More Time to recover between matches/games.

RUSH or speed decks are a good style of play for a new player:

  • Easier to find/purchase.
  • Can frequently brute force victories and forge keys via cheating so the opponent cannot respond with Amber Control

MID-RANGE

A solid performer. It may not be top tier but is still competitive enough for archon solo or best of three.

The majority of Keyforge decks are in this middling area.

A skilled player could go 4/4 with this type of deck in a sealed or casual environment

A good example midrange deck would be a Brobnar / Shadows / Untamed deck from set 1 aka Call of the Archons (CotA) with an equal focus on Fighting, Stealing, and Rushing.

GROUP HUG

The primary goal of a group hug deck is to make sure everyone gets to have fun and do cool things with their deck. Usually with a focus on symmetrical benefits that lead into a sneaky win condition.
