THE ECOSYSTEM OF CARD GAMES

Keyforge was and is methodone for former Magic the Gathering players. A game by Richard Garfield with a fresh take on the card game experience.

Less Deck Building, More Gaming.

Because of this it is impossible to talk about KEYFORGE without talking about games that exist(ed) before and after it.

At its launch the ways that Keyforge set out to fix the TCG/LCG/Card Battling experience were exciting.

OUT were detractors like:

FORMATS

🔥 ARCHON

There is no CONSTRUCTED in keyforge but this is the closest thing.

  • Official Format
  • Each player brings a deck to use at the event
  • You get to see your opponents deck list pre-game
  • Best-Of-One (generally)
  • 35 min rounds
  • Chainbound (generally)

Even quality keyforge decks can be really challenging to pilot so this has been the most enjoyable format for me because it allows the most practice ahead of tournament.


In general "spike" or competitive players are drawn to this format. Similar to "standard" in Magic the Gathering.

Because of the lack of deckbuilding and the scarcity/opinionated-ness around what makes a strong deck or player...some spike players may actually gravitate towards more intense formats like reversal or adapative!

🧠 ADAPTIVE

Best of 3 format iteration with chain bidding based on outcomes.

Each player brings an archon or sealed deck and plays normally in a best of 3.

For the second game, each player swaps decks with his or her opponent and plays using his or her opponent‚Äôs deck.

If the third game occurs, players bid chains to use the deck which won both of the previous games.

Other formats include TRIAD, Competitive Reversal, Adaptive-Short (Adaptive with only 1 round).

RUSH

Sonic the hedgehog. Superman. A Speeding Bullet. Ferrari. These decks floor it and don't look back. This an interesting strategy for a number of reasons:

  • Make all other strategies irrelevant by simply being faster.
  • Requires Less Cognitive lift at a tournament (compared to a more board or trick heavy deck.
  • Wins Faster. More Time to recover between matches/games.

MID-RANGE

A solid performer. It may not be top tier but is still competitive enough for archon solo or best of three.

The majority of Keyforge decks are in this middling area.

A skilled player could go 4/4 with this type of deck in a sealed or casual environment

A good example midrange deck would be a Brobnar / Shadows / Untamed deck from set 1 aka Call of the Archons (CotA) with an equal focus on Fighting, Stealing, and Rushing.
