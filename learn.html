🔥 ARCHON FORMAT

Each player brings a deck to use at the event.


In general "spike" or competitive players are drawn to this format. Similar to "standard" in magic the gathering" except there is no rotation or bannings

Because of the lack of deckbuilding and the scarcity/opinionated-ness around what makes a strong deck or player...some spike players may actually gravitate towards more intense formats like reversal or adapative!

🧠 ADAPTIVE FORMAT

A more skill intensive iteration with chain bidding based on outcomes.

Each player brings an archon or sealed deck and plays normally in a best of 3.

For the second game, each player swaps decks with his or her opponent and plays using his or her opponent’s deck.

If the third game occurs, players bid chains to use the deck which won both of the previous games.

Other formats include TRIAD, Competitive Reversal, Adaptive-Short (Adaptive with only 1 round).

GROUP HUG

The primary goal of a group hug deck is to make sure everyone gets to have fun and do cool things with their deck. Usually with a focus on symmetrical benefits that lead into sneaky win condition.
