JANK

[ˈjaNGkē] ADJECTIVE jank (adjective) of extremely poor or unreliable quality.

"The card player's suspect strategy was pretty janky."

Quality is subjective not quantitative. I go out of my way to build jank decks, and collect jank cards.

Simply by the way keyforge was designed as a unique deck game with decklists generated by an algorithm it acts as the ideal playground for the quirkiness and chaos of jank.

Literally every deck is unique.

So you can go to a sealed or constructed (called Archon in Keyforge) and never play the deck twice.

  • Weird Lists
  • Gems with combos that pay out little
  • Shells for engines that go nowhere
  • strange names
  • more

Any TCG or LCG can follow a meta list or be optimized. This is about more than the lack of amber control...some are LEGIT chase decks (multiples, combos, odd mavericks, decks that tell a story, etc).

JANK is about finding decks with unique and quirky gameplay opportunities.

A common misconception is that too many rares dilute the quality of a keyforge deck. Here is a counter example with 2 Scowly Capers that attack your opponent's board state like a pincer move.
